About

Bridging methods
and equity

I am a PhD student in Global Health Metrics and Implementation Science at the University of Washington and a Graduate Research Assistant at the Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation (IHME), where I develop small area estimation models for systolic blood pressure and hypertension at the US county level.

Before returning to academia, I spent a decade as an epidemiologist across Myanmar, Seattle, and Washington State — leading NCD policy programs at HelpAge International, building Tribal health equity infrastructure at WA DOH, and providing analytical consultation to Urban Indian Organizations nationwide.

My work sits at the intersection of advanced statistical methods (TMB, Bayesian hierarchical models, spatial analysis) and deeply applied public health — I care as much about what the estimates mean for communities as about how they were produced.

Research & Projects

Selected work

TMB Bayesian IHME

Small Area Estimation of Systolic Blood Pressure

Developing TMB-based latent log-normal models (Models 4–6) to estimate mean SBP and hypertension prevalence at the US county level by race/ethnicity and education. Models incorporate BRFSS, Medicare, NHANES, and MarketScan data with spatial and temporal random effects.

Key innovation: Heteroscedastic SD model links variability to mean SBP, enabling full distributional estimation including proportion above clinical thresholds.
View on GitHub →
R · JAGS Bayesian NHANES

Bayesian HbA1c Analysis — Sex, Age & Diabetes

Bayesian hierarchical model for HbA1c using NHANES data. Iterated from normal to log-normal likelihood with heteroscedastic group-specific residual SDs, incorporating self-reported diabetes diagnosis. Prior specification based on clinical domain knowledge.

Finding: Sex offset β₂ = 0.073 credibly above zero; sex × age interaction not credible after correcting σ prior — honest inference from proper prior specification.

Tribal Health Equity Dashboard

Built county-level chronic disease health equity ArcGIS Online dashboard for Washington State DOH. Developed epidemiology best practices guidance for reporting Tribal data and multiple racial/ethnic identities. Led cross-sector data equity workgroup.

Impact: Adopted by WA DOH as standard for Tribal data reporting. Delivered data sovereignty training to all DOH epidemiology staff.
